What could Orchestrators earn under Solstice?

Solstice pays registered Orchestrators a share of every block reward. That service share (w2) grows only when quarterly on-chain deal volume clears a rising target. Set a scenario to see the split, the gate, and what an Orchestrator earns.

Operating model Rewards are set by mechanism, not discretion: governance decides who is registered and how the service share is split — never the weights, and it can't hand-pick payouts. f02 pays each Orchestrator directly every epoch.

How this is calculated

Every formula and constant below is taken directly from the Solstice FIP draft (Service Rewards Actor & Stream Weight Actor specification). Values are the FIP's frozen illustrative parameters; swap in final governance-set numbers as they land.

Consensus weight (w1) & the service ceiling (α)

Consensus w1 declines on a deterministic, ungated linear ramp from 95% to a 50% floor over ~9 quarters. The service ceiling α = 1 − w1 is what's available to service. Because the ramp is continuous, the reward earned over a quarter uses the ceiling averaged across that quarter:

α_avg(Q) = min(0.50, 0.025 + 0.05·Q) → consensus w1 = 1 − α_avg Q1 7.5% · Q2 12.5% · Q3 17.5% · Q4 22.5% … reaching the 50% cap around Q10

Service weight (w2) & burn (w0)

  • Q1 is the ungated bootstrap: w2 tracks the ceiling (earns ~7.5% avg), nothing burns.
  • Entering Q2, w2 resolves to 10%.
  • Q2 onward: QuarterlyGateCheck reads the just-ended quarter's FPV; a pass raises w2 by 5pp, capped by the ceiling at quarter end.
  • Burn residual: w0 = 1 − w1 − w2 = α_avg − w2 — a missed gate's cost shows up the following quarter, as the ceiling ramps on while w2 stays put.

The volume gate

AggregatedFPV(Q−1) ≥ ComputeTarget(w2) ComputeTarget(w2) = 3,500 · 2.7^steps, steps = (w2 − 10%) / 5%, clamped 0–7

Frozen USD schedule: $3.5K, $9.45K, $25.5K, $68.9K, $186K, $502K, $1.36M, $3.66M for the eight steps (10→15% … 45→50%). Targets multiply ~2.7× per step; calibrated to Filecoin Pay actuals at FIL = $1.

Filecoin Pay Volume from storage

FPV(Q) = rate ($/TiB/mo) · capacity (TiB) · 3 months · (1 + growth)^(Q−1)

FPV is gross on-chain deal value settled through Filecoin Pay, scoped to an Orchestrator's registered (payer, operator) pairs. Here we approximate it from paid capacity and a revenue rate so the community can reason in storage terms.

Rewards

service stream = w2 · block_reward_per_day this Orchestrator = service stream / number of Orchestrators

The FIP divides w2 among Orchestrators in proportion to their FPV (the SRA's SplitRule, written via SetShares). This tool models an equal split across the chosen number of Orchestrators, so the headline is a representative per-Orchestrator payout (exact at N = 1, the Phase-1 launch). A failed per-Orchestrator send burns that share — it is never held or clawed back — and the registry is bounded by MAX_RECIPIENTS.

Who controls what

Two governance bodies, one per contract, each two independent org multisigs that both approve and either can cancel. Allocation Governance over the Stream-Split Controller (SWA) sets the weights and gate (per-change FIP + 7-day hold). Registry Governance over the SRA (FF + FilOZ, 2-of-2) runs admissions, disputes, freezes, and volume corrections under REGISTRY_CANCEL_HOLD — no per-change FIP. The quarterly gate step and SetShares recompute run permissionlessly; no one signs them.

How this is calculated

The Storage-Provider view is grounded in FIP-0118 (Solstice). SPs earn from two independent channels: block rewards (protocol) and client deal payments (market).

1. Block-reward income (consensus stream w1)

All PoRep sectors receive 10× QAP uniformly from the activation epoch, realized at onboarding regardless of content (Fil+/DataCap is retired). Because the multiplier is uniform, an SP's share of the consensus stream tracks its share of raw power:

block-reward income = w1(Q) · block_reward_per_day · (your capacity ÷ network power) w1(Q) = 1 − α_avg(Q): 95% → 50% floor over ~9 quarters (a fixed ramp)

Crucially, w1 is a fixed schedule — it does not depend on the volume gate. So every SP's direct block-reward income declines by the non-consensus share (1 − w1) as the service stream ramps. This is the intended shift: "all PoRep SPs see their direct block-reward income reduced by (1 − w1)."

2. Client deal revenue

SPs do not receive service-stream payouts directly; they "recover this through deal payments." We model deal revenue from the paid fraction of capacity and a per-TiB rate, growing over time as the SP wins demand:

deal revenue/day = (capacity · paid%) · rate ($/TiB/mo) · 12/365 · (1 + growth)^(Q−1)

Only paid data earns deal revenue; committed capacity earns block rewards only. The FIP's thesis: "SPs who serve clients recover [the loss] through deal payments; SPs who do not see a permanent reduction (the intended outcome)."

3. Total & the crossover

total SP revenue = block-reward income + client deal revenue

The headline is total USD/day (block rewards converted at the current FIL price). The mix chart shows the transition: block rewards shrink on the w1 ramp while deal revenue grows — an SP stays whole only by replacing block-reward income with real demand.

4. Net vs the status quo (the headline)

block reward given up = (1 − w1) · block_reward · your-power-share · FIL price net vs today = client deal revenue − block reward given up

Pre-Solstice, block reward went entirely to miners; Solstice hands (1 − w1) of it to the service economy or burn. That reduction is the SP's "loss." The net banner compares it head-to-head with the new deal revenue: whenever an SP earns more from paid deals than it gives up to the split, it comes out ahead of today. Because block reward per TiB is small relative to paid-storage rates, even a modest paid-deal book more than offsets the split — which is the FIP's intended outcome. (Simplification: the loss is measured against a same-power baseline; a CC-heavy SP's share actually rises as its sectors move to 10× QAP, so this is conservative.)

Pledge (context)

The initial-pledge formula is unchanged; only its input changes. With 10× QAP, pledge per raw byte scales up (more locked FIL), and expected reward scales with it, so return on pledge is roughly preserved — converging to today's verified-sector economics, already the norm for ~83% of the network. This tool models revenue, not locked-capital costs.
