How this is calculated

Every formula and constant below is taken directly from the Solstice FIP draft (Service Rewards Actor & Stream Weight Actor specification). Values are the FIP's frozen illustrative parameters; swap in final governance-set numbers as they land.

Consensus weight (w1) & the service ceiling (α)

Consensus w1 declines on a deterministic, ungated linear ramp from 95% to a 50% floor over ~9 quarters. The service ceiling α = 1 − w1 is what's available to service. Because the ramp is continuous, the reward earned over a quarter uses the ceiling averaged across that quarter:

α_avg(Q) = min(0.50, 0.025 + 0.05·Q) → consensus w1 = 1 − α_avg Q1 7.5% · Q2 12.5% · Q3 17.5% · Q4 22.5% … reaching the 50% cap around Q10

Service weight (w2) & burn (w0)

  • Q1 is the ungated bootstrap: w2 tracks the ceiling (earns ~7.5% avg), nothing burns.
  • Entering Q2, w2 resolves to 10%.
  • Q2 onward: QuarterlyGateCheck reads the just-ended quarter's FPV; a pass raises w2 by 5pp, capped by the ceiling at quarter end.
  • Burn residual: w0 = 1 − w1 − w2 = α_avg − w2 — a missed gate's cost shows up the following quarter, as the ceiling ramps on while w2 stays put.

The volume gate

AggregatedFPV(Q−1) ≥ ComputeTarget(w2) ComputeTarget(w2) = 3,500 · 2.7^steps, steps = (w2 − 10%) / 5%, clamped 0–7

Frozen USD schedule: $3.5K, $9.45K, $25.5K, $68.9K, $186K, $502K, $1.36M, $3.66M for the eight steps (10→15% … 45→50%). Targets multiply ~2.7× per step; calibrated to Filecoin Pay actuals at FIL = $1.

Filecoin Pay Volume from storage

FPV(Q) = rate ($/TiB/mo) · capacity (TiB) · 3 months · (1 + growth)^(Q−1)

FPV is gross on-chain deal value settled through Filecoin Pay, scoped to an Orchestrator's registered (payer, operator) pairs. Here we approximate it from paid capacity and a revenue rate so the community can reason in storage terms.

Rewards

service stream = w2 · block_reward_per_day this Orchestrator = (its FPV share) · service stream

In Phase 1 the single Orchestrator's share is 100%. In Phase 2, w2 is divided among Orchestrators in proportion to their FPV (the SRA's SplitRule, written via SetShares). A failed per-Orchestrator send burns that share — it is never held or clawed back — and the registry is bounded by MAX_RECIPIENTS.

Who controls what

Two governance bodies, one per contract, each two independent org multisigs that both approve and either can cancel. Allocation Governance over the Stream-Split Controller (SWA) sets the weights and gate (per-change FIP + 7-day hold). Registry Governance over the SRA (FF + FilOZ, 2-of-2) runs admissions, disputes, freezes, and volume corrections under REGISTRY_CANCEL_HOLD — no per-change FIP. The quarterly gate step and SetShares recompute run permissionlessly; no one signs them.
